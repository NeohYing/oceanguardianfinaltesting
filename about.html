About Us

Who We Are & What We Aim To Do

Our Mission

Our mission is to educate and inspire people to protect marine life and marine ecosystems by raising awareness of the challenges facing our oceans and encouraging sustainable actions that create a healthier, cleaner, and more sustainable future for generations to come.

Why We Created This

We created Ocean Guardians because our oceans are facing increasing threats, yet many people are unaware of how everyday actions can affect marine life and marine ecosystems. We believe that raising awareness is the first step toward inspiring responsibility, encouraging sustainable choices, and protecting our oceans for future generations.

About SDG 14

Our website is aligned with Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water, promoting marine conservation, protecting marine life and marine ecosystems, and encouraging sustainable actions. By educating and inspiring individuals to make environmentally responsible choices, it also supports Quality Education (SDG 4).

Our Objectives

  • Raise awareness of marine life.
  • Educate about marine ecosystems.
  • Highlight ocean threats.
  • Encourage sustainable actions.
  • Inspire people ocean conservation.
Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Our Oceans

1. Why should we care about the ocean?

The ocean covers over 70% of Earth's surface and provides food, oxygen, and livelihoods for billions of people. It also regulates the climate and supports a rich diversity of marine life, making it essential for life on Earth.

2. How much of the ocean has been explored?

Although the ocean covers most of our planet, only about 5% has been explored. Much of the deep sea remains a mystery, with many species and habitats still undiscovered.

3. Can marine life recover after being threatened?

Yes. With conservation efforts such as reducing pollution, protecting habitats, and practicing sustainable fishing, many marine species and ecosystems can gradually recover over time.

4. Why is the ocean important for climate?

The ocean absorbs heat and carbon dioxide, helping to regulate Earth's climate. Healthy oceans play a vital role in reducing the impacts of climate change.

5. Why is ocean conservation important?

Ocean conservation helps protect marine life, preserve biodiversity, and maintain healthy ecosystems. It also ensures that future generations can continue to benefit from the ocean's resources.

6. What is SDG 14: Life Below Water?

SDG 14: Life Below Water is one of the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. It aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources by reducing marine pollution, protecting marine ecosystems, promoting sustainable fishing, and safeguarding marine biodiversity for future generations.
